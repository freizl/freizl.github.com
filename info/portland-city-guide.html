1. Things to Do

  • Forest Park
    • Beautiful day hiking spot with hikes of varying difficulty. Most of it is shaded, so it will be wet and slippery if it’s raining but great on dry days. Easy to get lost so plan to be there longer than you think you will be. Pittock Mansion at the top of the park has a great view of Portland Metro.
  • Lan Sun Garden
  • Powell’s City of Books
    • The world’s largest independent bookstore. New & used books, and a rare book vault. (downtown)
  • NW 23rd - Shop, eat dinner, grab Salt & Straw!
  • The Japanese gardens
  • The rose gardens
  • Brewery tours / pub crawls
  • Visit food truck pods
  • Local coffee shops galore
  • Consignment shopping on Alberta & Hawthorne Street
  • Multnomah Falls
  • Trail of Ten Falls
  • Berry picking on Sauvie Island
  • Coast trip!

2. Notable Tourist Attractions

  • International Rose Test Garden. Seasonal for In the late spring-early fall. 600+ different types of roses grow here, including some that are experimental roses. Great city views as well.
  • Hiking in the Columbia River Gorge About 30 miles outside Portland there is a group of fine viewpoints and hikes along the Columbia River Gorge. Great viewpoints/hikes include the Vista House, Beacon Rock (on the Washington side), and several waterfall hikes on the Oregon side, the most famous of which is Multnomah Falls. If you have time, stop at the Bonneville dam fish hatchery to see Herman the Sturgeon, or see the salmon runs.
